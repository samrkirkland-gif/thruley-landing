The problem

One missed step can cost the commission,
not just the deal.

Queensland real estate runs on strict, shifting compliance: Form 6 appointments, Form 2 disclosures, trust accounting, cooling-off, CPD. Under the Property Occupations Act, a single defective Form 6 means no valid appointment and no entitlement to commission on the sale. The rules keep changing, and most agents still track every step by hand, from memory, at 10pm.

Under the hood

From listing to settlement. Here's what actually happens.

You win the listing day 0

Thruley builds your Form 6 and checks every required field before it goes out, watches the 90-day term, orders the Form 2 seller disclosure pack (title search, plan and certificates take 3 to 7 business days, so they're ready before any buyer signs) and starts the vendor identity check your AML obligations now require.

Campaign prep week 1

Photographer and floor plan booked from your calendar. Listing copy drafted in your voice for realestate.com.au and Domain. Vendor kickoff ready for your approval. Every ad checked against price-representation rules before it runs.

Live on market

Enquiry follow-ups drafted, open homes scheduled, and the vendor report goes out every Friday in your voice. Nothing waits for you to remember it.

Offer accepted

Buyer identity check runs (AML), the Form 2 disclosure and Form 8 buyer disclosure are attached before signing, the deposit is receipted into trust and banked by the next business day, and cooling-off timers start counting.

Under contract

Finance and building-and-pest deadlines tracked with nudges to the buyer's solicitor. Trust ledger stays reconciled. You see exactly where the deal stands.

Settlement

Final checklist closed out, review request sent, and the whole file, every step logged and timestamped, archived for the 7-year record-keeping you're required to hold.

Lives in the tools you already use: Gmail and Outlook, your calendar, SMS, and works alongside your CRM (AgentBox, VaultRE, Rex and others). Your CRM stays the source of truth.

Questions agents ask

The honest answers.

Does it replace my CRM?

No. Your CRM stays the source of truth. Thruley does the work around it: the follow-ups, the drafts, the bookings, the checks. It's designed to work alongside AgentBox, VaultRE, Rex and others.

Can it send something without me knowing?

No. Everything Thruley drafts sits in your approval queue. Anything compliance-touching or client-facing needs your explicit sign-off before it moves. That rule is structural, not a setting.

Will my clients know I'm using it?

Your clients only ever see you. Messages go from your address, in your voice, under the assistant name you choose. There's no Thruley branding on anything a vendor or buyer sees.

What does it cost?

The founding-partner pilot is free. You help us shape the product around your process, and you lock in founding pricing for life when we launch. A full-time PA costs $70–90k a year; Thruley will always be a fraction of that.

I'm a solo agent. Is this for me?

Especially for you. Solo agents carry the full admin load alone. Agencies get the oversight layer on top; solo agents get their evenings back.

Is Thruley endorsed by AUSTRAC or the OFT?

No, and be wary of anything that claims to be, because regulators don't endorse or certify software. What we do instead: every check is mapped to the specific rule that requires it, with the source named, and updated when the rules change. That's the coverage list up the page.

Is this legal advice?

No. Thruley automates workflows and runs checks against current requirements, and it's built to keep you inside the lines. It doesn't replace your judgement, your training, or your solicitor.
